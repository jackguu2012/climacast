What's new is ClimaCast 3?

By Jack Hambelton-Waite | 14th March 2025

ClimaCast v3 is the next major update to the ClimaCast website, introducing an all-new design that promotes a clean, minimalistic theme allowing you to get you're weather information quicker and easier.

One of the biggest things we've changed in v3 is our weather system. We've completely reimagined how you get your weather, now with real-time weather conditions and temperature, replacing what was an overview for your wider area. You can now search your location, for example "Westminster" which would have before been classified as "South of England." This change allows hyper-local forecasts, so you know exactly what your weather's doing.

We've revamped our other weather pages too. We now have articles (like this one) about related to weather and climate. This article is an exception. We plan to add new articles when there is a weather event worth knowing about happening soon. Articles are also your go-to place to maybe learn something new about weather/climate since content such as this will be posted frequently.

We've also made significant changes to our Dev pages. You may have never heard of these before, since they used to be for troubleshooting and development to the site, but are now open to the public at dev.climacast.uk. There are several pages with technical information, but we also have an Outages page for checking if there are any known problems with ClimaCast and a Releases page, for checking what's new and seeing the continuous development of ClimaCast.

You may have noticed that there are more images on ClimaCast now. We are trying to increase the number of images seen on the website since images add more of a colourful and full feel to the website.


There you go. That's ClimaCast 3.0, a major update introducing a new weather system and new design language.
